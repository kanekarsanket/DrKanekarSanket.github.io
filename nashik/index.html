Welcome to Nashik

About Nashik

Nashik is a vibrant city in Maharashtra, India, situated at the foothills of the Western Ghats. Known as the "Wine Capital of India," it boasts a perfect blend of ancient heritage, natural beauty, and modern development.

Home to the sacred Kumbh Mela site and over 100 temples, Nashik offers spiritual seekers a profound experience. The city's vineyards have put India on the global wine map, while its rich history dates back to the Ramayana era.

City Beauty

Vineyards & Wine Tours

Nashik is India's largest wine producer. Visit Sula Vineyards, York Winery, and Soma Vine Village for scenic tours and wine tasting experiences amidst rolling hills.

Godavari River

The sacred Godavari River flows through Nashik, offering serene ghats, morning walks, and boat rides. The Trimbakeshwar Temple sits at its source.

Ancient Temples

Nashik houses over 100 temples including the famous Kalaram Temple, Kapaleshwar Temple, and the Panchavati area mentioned in the Ramayana.

Western Ghats

Surrounded by the stunning Western Ghats, Nashik offers trekking spots like Anjaneri Fort and stunning viewpoints for nature lovers.

Things to Do

🍷 Wine Tasting

Visit Sula Vineyards for wine tours, tasting sessions, and enjoy the grape festival. York Winery offers sunset views with wine.

🛕 Temple Pilgrimage

Explore Panchavati, Kalaram Temple, and Brahmagiri Hill. Take a holy dip at Ram Kund and visit the ancient Sita Gufa caves.

🚶 Trekking

Trek to Anjaneri Fort, Dhodap Fort, or the Pandavleni Caves. The Western Ghats offer trails for all adventure levels.

🛶 River Activities

Enjoy boating at Godavari ghats, especially during evenings. Visit the Nashik Epressway for scenic drives along the river.

🎭 Culture & History

Visit the Nashik Museum, explore British-era architecture, and experience the famous Nashik Fobia festival.

🛍️ Shopping

Explore MG Road, Satpur Main Road for shopping. Don't miss Nashik's famous grapes, wine, and handcrafted items.
